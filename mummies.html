The Art of Mummification

Mummification was a process used by ancient Egyptians to preserve the body for the afterlife. It was a complex ritual that took 70 days.

The 5 Main Steps:

  1. Purification: The body is washed with water from the Nile and palm wine.
  2. Organ Removal: Internal organs (except the heart) are removed and stored in Canopic Jars.
  3. Drying: The body is covered in Natron salt for 40 days to absorb all moisture.
  4. Wrapping: The body is tightly wrapped in layers of linen bandages.
  5. Opening of the Mouth: A final ritual performed by priests to restore the senses for the afterlife.

Materials Used:

  • Linen strips
  • Natron (natural salt)
  • Pine resin
  • Amulets for protection
